    CHAPTER FOUR: NONSENSE THE LONE PUPPY

    Chocolate had gone. She had taken huge chunk of my heart with her. Now there only remained Nonsense, the embodiment of happiness, love, peskiness and mischief. She would run around the complex twenty-four hours a day. She was cute, with large, adorable eyes and floppy ears like her Labrador-resembling father. Her mother simply adored her. But another dark cloud overshadowed the dogs. Soon, Nonsense was infected by Parbo-virus, the lethal disease which had robbed Chocolate of her jubilant life. Luckily, everyone was aware and on the alert this time. Nonsense was given injections and medication. But she would not eat. I tempted her with large Good Day biscuits, but she ignored them. Bimal Uncle mixed biscuit crumbs with water and filled up a syringe with this curious liquid. He inserted the syringe between the teeth of a reluctant Nonsense, who had been quietly sunning herself. She sucked in the mixture of water and biscuit. A few days later, I went to visit Nonsense. She was playing in front of the office with her mother. Nonsense looked as happy and springy as ever. She had a new red collar around her neck. She darted over to meet me. She was cured. I regretted how nobody realised Chocolate’s illness. Now Nonsense was cured, she would start licking everybody and pirouetting around again.

    Nonsense is in her canine preadolescence now. Her floppy ears have perked up. She constantly troubles Sweetie for food. She goes and pokes her mother and pushes her. In the afternoon, she lazily lies in the office. I often go and pet her. Then she bares her teeth, lifts her leg and widens her eyes. It is her way of expressing happiness. She, along with her old father, fat mother and skinny stepsister, guard the complex at night. They bark, yowl, howl and wail. Socrates makes his special whines. Sweetiepie has a special way of howling. She howls at the moon, like the foxes. She must have learned it from her vulpine cousins (I am speaking scientifically. The dogs chase the foxes around all the time). Just last night, Sweetie was seen giving howling classes to Nonsense. She was showing to which direction Nonsense must point her nose at. I don’t know why Sweetie admires vulpine howling.

    Nonsense mysteriously disappeared one day. Sunil Uncle said that all four dogs had come down to Plato the Raider's territory, the Western Raider Road. I supposed that Nonsense might have been lost in a narrow alley or gotten kidnappped or pursued by The Raider. Almost a month after Nonsense had vanished, when the green auto that'd take me to school had come, I found a thin, scrawny puppy nosing me. I inspected the cinnamon fur, squint-eyes, multicolored tail and white nose-mark. It was Nonsense, but what an avatar of her! It seemed to be the very skeleton of my darling puppy. Maybe she'd gotten lost in a maze of roads, lanes and alleys. Maybe she'd survived upon only the slushy puddles that the heavy monsoon rain was forming on the roads, causing her appearance as though the skin had been tied to her body. Her rib cages were showing through the now-translucent skin. I had to go to school. When I returned home under the radiant, scorching hot afternoon sun, I saw Nonsense sitting near the closed grill-gates of my house. She was ecstatic to see me, as always, but the joyfully orotund bark had metamorphosed into a cough-stricken sparrow's unsteady chirrup. I just had to care for Nonsense aka Chocopie (that is what we complex kids call her; I named her Nonsense). I gave her four biscuits. I was astounded at the disgraceful behavior of old Socrates. When I gave Non two Marie Gold biscuits, he came up and growled at his own daughter to leave the food to him and scoot. While he lets Sam do what she likes. Just think! I just hope Sweetie, her mother, will be kind to her. A bit later, Sam approached Nonsense and yapped, falsely thinking she was the Princess-Majesty of 79 Tilla. Non soon proved her very wrong, by chasing her away and pushing her into a little ditch. Now, we, the children of our complex, comprising of Dabbhai, Omdada and I, raised a contribution to give biscuits to Nonsense. Omdada stood guard over Socrates while I fed Nonsense, and occasionally gave a few pieces to Samolina, as well. Socrates is becoming the King Greediest, just as Sam is Princess Mischief. Why should he be so bad? Maybe he did not recognize his daughter through the half-blind, milky, cloudy-grey eyes. Non has some wounds on her tail and paws, which might have been the result of an attack and chase by Plato. I have promised Nonsense that I'll get her back into the healthy avatar in a week.

    Samolina and Nonsense are having a horrible arguement. Nonsense is thinking that she is the daughter by birth of Old Dog Socrates and rightful heiress of 79 Tilla and that Sam is just a little picked-up puppy. While Sam is yapping out with all the might of her strange voice and slim, black body that Nonsense left her home and went away, so why should the other puppy not be here? They often fight. Once, Nonsense pushed Sam into Rina Aunty's beautiful row of plastic flowerpots which contained eye-catching orchids, petunias and hydrangeas. Samolina nipped Non on her nose.
