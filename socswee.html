    CHAPTER ONE: SOCRATES AND SWEETIEPIE

    Socrates is the patriarch of the dogs. He has floppy Labrador ears, hanging jowls and two large milky (he’s half-blind) emotional eyes. His back and tail seems all chewed-up. That was not the case two years ago. So when did his fur putrefy, I hear you ask. Who chewed Soc’s fur? He chewed it himself. He chewed and chewed. His fur is full of nits and ticks. Of course, it itches like mad. His back looks bilious and his tail looks thin. But he is a good dog. At day, he is an old, feeble dog who is sleeping diurnally. At evening, he is a howling, edacious tyrant who tries to snatch food from my mother’s hands. He is lenient with his family in general, but is firm upon one point: FOOD. We give the dogs food in newspapers. Whenever Socrates even moves toward Nonsense during feeding time, she quietly slinks away. Socrates Whines sound like wails. When Soc is getting impatient about when his dinner is going to come, he sits in front of our door and lets out those special yowls of his. He has a stink from the wound on his back. It smells horrible. That’s why my mother wants to shut him out of the apartment. It can’t be helped. Nobody can stand the disgusting odour of Socrates’ back. He sleeps more often now. He can’t run fast, or jump around, or quarrel with Plato. But he sleeps well and eats well.

    He has a lasting enmity with Plato the Raider, a dirty street dog with a coarse attitude. Soc still seems frightening and big to Plato, I think, so the younger dog's overconfidence is a bit halted and he does not really attack him. But Soc’s once-ferocious snarls now crumble into creaky nasal whines. When The Raider comes, Nonsense rolls all over the road and wees herself in fear. She seems to pleading with Plato to leave Socrates alone. Sam sits five metres away and lets out high-pitched barks. Plato is a large dog, younger and more youthful then Socrates, with cinnamon-red fur, a lot of brawn and chutzpah and a black mark on his forehead. His growl is acerbic and cacophonous. He has his own territory by the road. He has a mate called Lhassa. Plato is mostly not at home. He goes out wandering and probably threatening other dogs like Socrates for their territories. His hench-dog, Cupcake, guards his territory. Cupcake is a red dog with a broad white blemish on his face. He does not seem as aggresive and goon-like as Plato. One day, Plato and Soc were threatening each other so much the situation seemed on the brink of a fight. And Socrates, although big, was much weaker than Plato. So Papa had to chase The Raider away with pebbles. But Plato the Raider is not the only enemy Socrates has. The canine owners of Memorial Math are Snobbs, a snub-nosed dog with Pitbull eyes, and his gang, Skinny, Howler, Barker and Skimpy the Stinker. Snobbs is belligerent and froward. He and his gang often fight with Socrates. Once we witnessed Snobbs' Gang in a tussle with Socrates. The five dogs rolled over and over. Clouds of dust were floating in the air. When the fight ended, Snobbs emerged victorious, with Socrates standing growling and surrounded by the Gang. Recently, I made friends with Barker, who is a large dog with a black nose and a serius expression. He was lying on the raised platform of Memorial Math. I gave him some cakes and petted him. He looked like a thinker, like Socrates. I gave food to Skinny and Howler also. I couldn't find Snobbs anywhere.

    Sweetiepie is Socrates’ mate. She is a flabby bitch with a motherly attitude towards her daughter, Nonsense. She developed an even caring demeanour after Nonsense nearly died of Parbo-virus, like her sister Chocolate. Sweetiepie allows Nonsense to eat her food. She loves chasing Cocky, the black cat who lives at the office. Since Sweetiepie is very gentle, she allows Nonsense and Socrates to eat her food. Last night, Sweetie did not come to eat her food. She was feeling angry because her mate and children always robbed her food. Papa had to go and feed her. Once, my family and I went on a twelve-day journey to Kolkata. The dogs were given food every day. Yet, when we got down from Bimal Uncle’s van, there was a marvellous canine welcome awaiting us. Nonsense howled and howled while Sam, the youngest, came up and licked everyone. Socrates let off a volley of squeaky whines and accusations. But Sweetie was the best. She twisted her hindquarters around, wagged her tail, waddled around and bayed louder than her darling daughter Nonsense (that’s her signature mark of affection). Then she leaped up and put her paws on my shoulders. I don’t know how she did that. She had never displayed this eccentric sign of welcome. Her face said welcome-back-what-did-you-mean-going-and-leaving-me-having-fun-give-love-right-now. Well, I just fled upstairs. Sweetie and Nonsense were standing right in front of the door. Sweetiepie has continued this tradition of embracing me since.
